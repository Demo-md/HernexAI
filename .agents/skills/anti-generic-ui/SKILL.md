# Anti-Generic UI

Use during creative direction, component design, and critique. Read `references/anti-pattern-catalogue.md` and `references/composition-alternatives.md`.

## Rules

- Allow at most one conventional card-grid section per page.
- Do not use a card when typography, spacing, a divider, a list, or a diagram communicates the information better.
- Do not place every section inside rounded containers or use large radiuses by default.
- Avoid randomly rotated, floating, staggered sloppy cards; make asymmetry structural and purposeful.
- Avoid meaningless glowing orbs, purple-blue AI gradients unless brand-required, generic dashboard fragments, and identical section structures.
- Avoid excessive center alignment. Prefer editorial layouts, sticky narratives, timelines, marquees, media frames, lists, diagrams, and typographic compositions.
- Use proof before hype. Keep human creativity more prominent than AI symbolism.

## Review Loop

For every proposed section, name its narrative job, content hierarchy, alternative composition considered, and why its decoration earns its cost. If the same pattern appears three times, redesign at least two instances.
